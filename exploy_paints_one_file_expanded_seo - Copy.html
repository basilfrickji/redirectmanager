Frequently Asked Questions

These answers help customers understand common painting service details before contacting Exploy Paints.

Do you provide estimates?

Customers may contact Exploy Paints to request an estimate. Estimate availability, response time, and final pricing depend on the project details, location, access, surface condition, and scope.

Can customers choose their own paint color?

Yes. Customers can choose preferred colors and finishes. Final color names, product lines, sheen levels, and room placement should be confirmed before work begins.

Do you repair wall damage?

Minor preparation may be available depending on the project. Larger drywall repair, water damage, mold, structural movement, or major surface problems may need a separate specialist before painting.

How long does painting take?

Timing depends on room count, surface condition, repairs, number of coats, paint type, drying time, weather for exterior projects, and customer access.

Can exterior painting be delayed by weather?

Yes. Rain, wind, humidity, temperature, and surface moisture can affect exterior painting. Rescheduling may be needed if conditions are not suitable.

How can customers contact Exploy Paints?

Customers can call +1-888-527-3405, email support@exploypaints.com, or use the contact page to start an estimate request.

Should I test paint colors before choosing?

Testing colors can be helpful because paint may look different on your wall than it does on a screen or small sample card. Lighting, flooring, furniture, wall texture, and time of day can all change the appearance. If the color decision is important, customers should consider sample testing before approving a full project.

What is the difference between paint finish options?

Paint finishes can include flat, matte, eggshell, satin, semi-gloss, and gloss depending on the product. Lower-sheen finishes often hide wall imperfections better, while higher-sheen finishes may be easier to clean but can show surface flaws more clearly. The right finish depends on the room, traffic level, moisture exposure, and desired appearance.

Do darker colors require more work?

Sometimes. Dark colors may require careful cutting, even coverage, and additional coats. Covering dark paint with a lighter color may also require primer or extra coats. Strong reds, blues, greens, and deep accent colors can sometimes need more product to look even.

Can painting cover stains?

Some stains may require special primer before painting. Water stains, smoke stains, marker stains, tannin bleed, and old discoloration can sometimes show through standard paint if not properly treated. The source of the stain should also be reviewed, especially if moisture is involved.

What should I move before painters arrive?

Customers should move small items, fragile objects, wall decor, electronics, personal items, and anything that blocks access to the work area. Furniture movement should be discussed during the estimate so both sides know what is expected.

Can painting be done while I am living in the home?

Often, yes, depending on the project. Customers should discuss access, room sequence, drying time, ventilation, pets, children, and daily routines before work begins. Larger projects may require planning so rooms remain usable when possible.
